National Basketball Association

The National Basketball Association, or NBA, is a professional basketball league composed of 30 teams across North America featuring the best basketball players in the world. The NBA is a collection of 30 teams, broken down by two conferences with three divisions apiece. The Western Conference plays host to the Northwest, Pacific and Southwest Divisions. The Eastern Conference's 15 squads also break up across three divisions: the Atlantic, Southeast and Central.

The National Basketball Association was founded at the Commodore Hotel in New York. Maurice Podoloff was the league's first president, a title later changed to commissioner. Eleven teams were part of that league, orginally called the Basketball Association of America. Podoloff's name is now emblazoned on the NBA MVP trophy.

How many teams make the Playoffs?

The top eight teams in each Conference reach the postseason, with the No. 1 seed facing No. 8 in the first round, No. 2 vs No. 7 and so on. Each round features a best-of-seven series with a 2-2-1-1-1 format (two games at the top seed's home court, two games at the lower seed's home, then back and forth one game at a time).

What is the Playoff Play-in Tournament?

The NBA Board of Governors approved a proposal to implement a Playoff Play-In Tournament. The Play-In Tournament will determine the teams that will fill the seventh and eighth playoff seeds in each conference.
